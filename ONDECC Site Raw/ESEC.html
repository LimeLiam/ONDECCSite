Elementary Science and Engineering Challenge E.S.E.C.

What we do as elementary aged students can have lasting effects on the rest of our lives. That is why the ONDECC Project is committed to making a difference in the world of elementary education by designing and operating events for elemtary school students through ESEC.

The Elementary Science and Engineering Challenge (ESEC) program was started in fall of 2019 as a pilot program and is now one of our most successful established programs to date. The aim of ESEC is to inspire, encourage, and expose elementary school students to the foundational elements of STEM innovation as well as other fundamental skills such as presentation and leadership. It fosters independent problem-solving skills that allows students to achieve higher standards of success in all areas including those outside of STEM.

During an ESEC event, elementary students will be presented with a challenge (ex. Engineering a parachute design, engineering a bridge design, constructing a tower, etc). From this, they will take classes on certain subject areas and utilize their knowledge and skills to engineer a solution to the presented problem. They will then present their ideas and solutions and possibly receive awards.
